# E1c Protocol — Think-Aloud Guide
# Moderator Script for Borg CLI Usability Sessions

---

## Before the Session

**Greet the participant:**
> "Thank you for joining today. I'm [Name], and I'll be moderating this session. We're evaluating Borg, a CLI tool for AI agents. Your feedback will help us improve its usability."

**Review consent:**
> "Before we begin, did you get a chance to review the consent form? Do you have any questions? And you consent to being recorded?"

**Set up recording:**
- Confirm screen recording + audio capture is active
- Ensure participant's face is visible if using webcam for facial expressions

**Warm-up:**
> "Let me ask you a quick question: think about the last time you learned a new CLI tool. What made it easy or difficult to learn?"

---

## Session Introduction (Script)

Say to the participant:

> "In this session, I'll ask you to perform several tasks using Borg's command-line interface. As you work, I want you to **think aloud** — that means saying whatever comes to mind as you're using the tool. This includes:
> - What you're trying to do
> - What you expect to happen
> - What you see happening
> - Any confusion or frustration
> - What you would try next
>
> There are no right or wrong answers. We're evaluating the tool, not you. If you get stuck, that's valuable information — just tell me what you're thinking.
>
> Do you have any questions before we begin?"

**Wait for confirmation, then begin tasks.**

---

## Think-Aloud Prompts (For Moderator)

Use these when the participant goes silent or needs prompting:

### Neutral Prompts (use first)
- "What are you thinking about right now?"
- "What did you just do?"
- "What do you expect to happen if you run that?"
- "Can you tell me what you're looking at?"

### Encouraging Prompts (if hesitant)
- "Take your time — there's no rush."
- "You don't have to figure it out alone — just tell me what you're considering."
- "Any thought is helpful — even guesses."

### Refocus Prompts (if off-topic)
- "Let's shift back to the interface — what are you seeing on screen?"
- "Focusing on the tool itself, what are you noticing?"

### Probe Prompts (for rich data)
- "You paused there — what caught your attention?"
- "You said that was confusing — can you say more about that?"
- "Would you expect it to work differently? How?"
- "If you could change one thing right now, what would it be?"

## Handling Difficult Situations

### Participant is Very Silent
- Pair them with another participant for collaborative think-aloud
- Ask: "If you were explaining this to a colleague, what would you say?"
- Offer a simpler task

### Participant is Very Talkative (monopolizing)
- Use: "That's helpful context. Let's see what happens when you try the next step."
- Gently redirect: "Let's capture that idea — I'm curious about what you'd do here."

### Participant Gets Frustrated
- Acknowledge: "I can see that's frustrating — that's exactly the kind of feedback we need."
- Offer break: "Would you like to take a 5-minute break?"
- Remind: "This helps us find real problems."

### Participant Asks for Help
- Use the **Crusader technique**: Answer with a question
  - Instead of explaining, ask: "What do you think it should do?"
  - Or: "What would help you figure this out?"
- If stuck for >2 minutes, you may give minimal guidance (note this in your notes)

---

## Think-Aloud Best Practices for Moderators

1. **Never evaluate** — don't say "good" or "bad," "right" or "wrong"
2. **Stay neutral** — your facial expressions and tone should be consistent
3. **Listen more than you speak** — your job is to extract data, not provide it
4. **Don't teach** — if they make an error, note it and move on
5. **Capture exact quotes** — write down or record what they say verbatim
6. **Note non-verbal cues** — laughter, sighing, forehead furrowing, etc.

## Task Completion Checklist

For each task, note:
- [ ] Task completed successfully? (Yes / No / Partial)
- [ ] Time on task
- [ ] Number of prompts needed
- [ ] Critical incidents (confusion points, errors)
- [ ] Participant quotes (verbatim)

---

## Closing Script

After all tasks are complete:

> "That's the end of the tasks. I have a few quick follow-up questions, and then we're done."

**Post-task questions:**
1. "Overall, how would you describe your experience with Borg?"
2. "What was the most confusing part? The least confusing?"
3. "Would you use a tool like this? Why or why not?"
4. "If you could change one thing about the interface, what would it be?"

**Thank the participant:**
> "Thank you so much for your time today. Your feedback is incredibly valuable. [Explain compensation/next steps.]"

---

## Emergency Notes

If a participant becomes distressed or the session must be stopped:
1. End the session gracefully
2. Note the reason for stopping
3. Follow protocol for partial compensation if applicable
4. Report to study coordinator immediately
